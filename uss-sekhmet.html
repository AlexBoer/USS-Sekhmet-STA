USS Sekhmet

Summary

Location:: The Shackleton Expanse
Portrait::
Note:: Pulled back into service to supplement Starfleet's overextended fleet, the USS Sekhmet stands on guard over Narendra station and the Shackleton Expanse beyond.

Description

The USS Sekmet is a Columbia Class cruiser stationed at Narendra Station. After the bombings on the Mars Utopia Planitia shipyards, Starfleet supplemented it's fleet by pulling retired vessels back into service with substantial refits.

The Sekhmet carries a crew of a little over 100 crewman and officers.

Traits

  • Federation Starship
  • Columbia Class
  • Garbage Scow

Namesake

Sekhmet is the Egyptian goddess of war and medicine. The USS Sekhmet is the first of it's name, and was chosen to signal it's equal potential as a protector and threat.

Mission

The USS Sekhmet, has been assigned to Narendra station as an emergency response and tactical strike starship. While Sekhmet has a regular crew, the Narendra Station provides a rotating roster of commanding officers due to stretched personnel resources and the Sekhmet's irregular mission schedule.

Mission Logs

  • Sekhmet Log 1.1 - Bacchus' Irresistible Call:
    Stardate 67156, Session: 1
    The Sekhmet investgated a missing science team at Bacchus-3, only to get caught in the same subspace distortion wave and became stranded. On the planet, they found the team strange entranced by massive monoliths, even willing to die to get inside.
    The USS Sekhmet was sent to investigate the disappearance of a Starfleet science shuttle at the Bacchus system. When the Sekhmet arrived, it was violently thrown out of warp by a massive subspace distortion wave. With warp-speed rendered impossible and subspace communications completely washed out by background subspace frequencies in the system, they managed to push a probe far enoguh outside the system to get a distress signal out.

    Next, an away was sent down to Bacchus-3 to investigate the source of the distortion waves. A small coalition team of Starfleet and Klingon officers seemed entranced by the giant monoliths sticking out of the surface of the planet. Soon, the scientists opened the monoliths, revealing a facility on the inside. Two of the closest scientists threw themselves into the facility, getting vaporized instantly.
  • Sekhmet Log 1.2 - Escaping from Bacchus-3:
    Stardate 67156, Session: 2
    The Sekhmet's away team ventured into the spire on Bacchus-3, and found a message from an unknown alien race. They asked the away team to find "the facility". They disabled the subspace distortion wave, and evacuated from the planet and the system, bringing wanted Klingon pirates with them.
    The Sekhmet's away team managed to retrain the remaining survivors on Bacchus-3 before they ran into the spire's interior. Val mind-melded with the science team's vulcan officer, and found a repeating message in his subconscious: "Find the facility." Sorran Vek found that D'Lok, a Klingon, at a brainwave resonating with the subspace distortion frequency.

    Conrak phasered one of the two phaser arrays guarding the entrance, which activated a security system closing the door. Conrak rushed forward and held the door open while Val disabled the second one. The away team entered the spire, leaving a small security team to guard the restrained scientists.

    Inside, the engineering specialist Victoria noted the amounts of power running through the spire was no enormous there were very few practical uses for it all. But it was enough power to punch wormholes through space-time. At the center of the spire, the found a control room with consoles for security, power management, subspace field generation, and more. They disabled the security system first, and then turned off the subspace field generator.

    An automated hologram projection of an alien introduced themselves as representing the "Displaced." The implored the group to find something called "The Facility" but didn't know where it was. It said its people were in peril and asked for the away team to go save them. It directed the away to to collect a bread-box sized sandstone prism with runes carved along the outside.

    The automated messenger finally urged them to leave quickly, because their ship would be in danger. The spires of Bacchus-3 was tractoring the nearest planet towards it, ripping the planet apart as it went. It was rebuilding a new Spire to replace the disabled one. A meteor shower threatened to destroy the Sekhmet and a disabled Klingon vessel. The away team and others on the surface returned to the Sekhmet

    The Klingon vessel had arrived to find their "compatriots", and demanded to have them returned. Val convinced the Klingons that if they allowed the Sekhmet to tractor them out of the system to safety, then the fugitives would be returned, with an implication that they would be set free.

    However, the commanding officer Vrenta Ewasi instead ordered the Sekhmet to rendezvous with Starfleet vessels answering a distress signal. The Klingons were arrest, and the Sekhmet praised for not only saving the survey team, but also bringing in wanted criminals.
  • Sekhmet Log 2 - Peas to Meet You:
    Stardate 67197, Session: 3
    The Sekhmet assisted the USS Constantine in delivering a crucial agricultural exchange to a colony in the Bix system. The crew and passengers of the Constantine had succumbed to a neurotoxin produced by Ice Peas while not in it's natural habitat.
    The USS Sekhmet was sent to investigate the USS Constantine, a Galaxy Class starship itself tasked with transporting an agricultural exchange from the Colony on Re-Alpha to a struggling colony on Bix. Aboard the Sekhmet, a boarding party found the crew of the Constantine acting strangely. The Executive officer ambushed the party, seemingly paranoid that they were intruders. The Captain was engaged in some sort of wrestling tournament, and various other crew members were exhibiting strong emotional reactions.

    The boarding party found the crew was under the effects of a neurotoxin called Wexylin-5C, which would cause different reactions from each person as a stress response. They tracked down the source of this neurotoxin to a plan on board called Ice Peas. The colonists had brought the crop on board and were sharing the food with the crew. The crop had been removed from it's natural habitat and was creating this neurotoxin in response to losing a particular fungus in it's soil. The boarding party was able to reintroduce the fungus into the Ice Pea bushes soil substitute, and bring the crew back to normal.
  • Sekhmet Log 3 - Orion Heist:
    Stardate 67217, Session: 4
    The Sekhmet was sent to pursue an Orion freighter which had stolen the Bacchus Artifact. The chase lead to the Romulan neutral zone. Before the Sekhmet could tractor beam the freighter and head back to base, a Romulan Warbird decloaked. The Sekhmet disabled the freighter's shields, beamed the artifact aboard, and fled the neutral zone before the Warbird could break the Sekhmet's own shields.
    While Lt Sorran was studying the Bacchus artifact, Lt Kassandra Pruitt, suffered a massive neural failure cascade, sending her to the emergency room. Meanwhile the Artifact was stolen from the lab. Admiral Banna Stoker gave instructions to the Sekhmet to find it. The artifact could not be located on the station, and only 2 starships had left Narendra Station recently.

    The Sekhmet started by chasing down a Klingon Bird of Prey, who turned out to be innocent. This mistake meant an Orion Freighter made it all the way to the Romulan Neutral Zone before the Sekhmet caught up. In the neutral zone, they found the back end of the freighter caved in like an aluminum can, with some kind of gravity field compressing the ship's hull.

    Lt. Commander Val ordered the freighter to be hulled away with a tractor beam, but in the same moment a Romulan decloaked and caught the freighter in it's own tractor beam. This lead to a brief firefight. The Sekhmet disabled the Orion freighters shields and beamed the artifact aboard. The artifact was still attached to an Orion via a telepathic link which would kill him if removed forcefully.

    The the Orion freighter captain, Paljara, begged Val to send the artifact back, as it was ransom for her sister's life. Val refused to comply. The Romulan captain also threatened the Sekhmet, warning of dire consequences if the artifact and the Orion were not returned.

    In the end, the Sekhmet managed to charge it's warp drive and race back to Narendra station. Admiral Stoker commended the Sekhmet for a job well done. But as the Sekhmet retreated, the Romulans had destroyed the Orion freighter.
  • Sekhmet Log 4 - Imminent Collision:
    Stardate 67233, Session: 5
    The Sekhmet made first contact with a new species made of crystal that communicated with refracted light. They crew assisted the aliens by removing an unidentified torpedo from the hull, and sent them on their way.
    After Narendra Station picked up an unidentified object approaching the station at high warp speeds, the USS Sekhmet was sent to investigate while the station prepared for a potential attack. They found a strange crystalline starship and attempted rudimentary and cooperative communication with it.

    On board the crystal starship, the crew found an unidentified torpedo lodged into the side of the structure, seemingly made just for the purpose of attacking this kind of starship. As the hull of the ship seemed to be a living entity of some sort, an away team organized a surgical bomb disposal procedure to remove the torpedo safely. This triggered the torpedo to explode and it was sent into space with an emergency transport.

    Once the torpedo was removed, the strange crystal beings who communicated through light refraction departed back into the Shackleton Expanse.
  • Sekhmet Log 5.1 - The Pasqual Problem:
    Stardate 67255, Session: 6
    The Sekhmet picked up Admiral Stoker and was ordered to Pasqual-1. There, they found a planet cloaked in an electromagnetic field that interfered with sensors and would disable and electronic equipment. It was covered in hundreds of strange black obelisks, 3 stories tall, and was home to a pre-industrial civilization.
    Sorran and Val were continuing investigation into Bacchus Artifact by performing controlled mind melds into it. However, the artifact's signal overloaded the failsafe meant to protect Val. It was deemed unsafe to continue these kinds of investigations.

    A shuttle was found rushing out of the Shackleton Expanse and towards the Sekhmet. Admiral Stoker was aboard and had taken damage as if in a firefight. When she boarded, she ordered the Sekhmet to head towards the Pasqual system in the Shackleton. Investigation into the shuttle revealed that she had indeed gotten into a fight with an unknown civilization. Her shuttle had visited several systems in the Shackleton.

    After cleaning up, Stoker briefed the crew on their mission: Pasqual-1 was an M-Class planet with a strange electromagnetic field shielding it from sensors. Stoker expected this planet might have remnants from the same civilization as Bacchus. The crew was also to keep a lookout for enemy ships that may have followed Stoker's shuttle

    After the briefing, Stoker took Conrak and Val aside and made it clear that the civilian Hik was to be enlisted into the Starfleet command structure or be removed from the ship's crew. Hik would be given a suitable position for her experience.

    The Sekhmet arrived at Pasqual-1 and found it covered in approximately 500 black obsidian monoliths, each about 3 stories tall. Each one had a small settlement build around it from a pre-industrial civilization.

    The Sekhmet put together an away team for Pasqual-1, and beamed down even through the electromagnetic interference. They aimed several kilometers outside a settlement and intended to work their way in to investigate the monolith there. In the woods, the away team was attacked by 4 panther-like predators, which were then killed or scared away.

    On the way to the nearest monolith, the away team followed a river until they found a woman and two children by a waterfall. They were doing laundry. Each one looked to be a different species than the others altogether.
  • Sekhmet Log 5.2 - Monoliths:
    Stardate 67255, Session: 7
    Val, Conrak, Sorren, and Karnes explored Pasqual-1 and followed some locals back to a community built around a black monolith. They discovered that the monolith was performing genetic experiments and forcefully evolving the population, and then attempting to send it to the Seku system. The electromagnetic field seemed to be an unintended interaction of the monoliths and the planet's crust.
    Val, Conrak, Sorren, and Karnes approached the three locals of Pasqual-1 at the waterfall. After making introductions, Val was able to mind meld with the locals to communicate. The woman introduced herself as Zumala, and the two children were Tadbon and Klopflim. Val hid their true origins from Zumala during the mind meld, but Zumala was able to sense him holding back information.

    The away team followed the three Pasqualese into their town, and were introduced to their elder, Jasper. Sorren and Val investigated the monolith in the center of town while Conrak aquainted himself with the locals.

    During a shared dinner outdoors, the monolith lit up in blue lights and 12 people around it fell to the ground, convulsing as it electrocuted. The people of the village seemed worried about it, but expected it to happen.

    Val convinced Jasper to bring them underneath the monolith, which Jasper considered holy ground. But in the mind meld, Jasper gleaned that the away team with from the stars and interpreted that as the team being angels. He asked them to bring him with them to the stars and Val agreed (at least temporarily.)

    Underneath the monolith, the away team found a control room. After some investigation, they discovered the control room was attempting to send genetic information from the community out to the Seku system in the Shackleton Expanse. They also found that every 6 months or so, the tower would send a signal out to several members of the community to revise their DNA, and then their children would be born with new features.

    With this information, the away team decided to return to the transport pickup spot. They brought Jasper, but Jasper was left behind as they were beamed back onto the Sekhmet. The Sekhmet was in red alert, and under attack.
  • Sekhmet Log 5.3 - Vinshari Attack:
    Stardate 67255, Session: 8
    When the away team on Pasqual-1 returned to the Sekhmet, it was under attack from a starship crewed by the Vinshari, a new civilization. Conrak and Val were able to work efficiently as a team and quickly disabled the starship. The Vinshari claimed that the Admiral had stolen from them, and demanded the Admiral be handed over to them to face justice. In the end, Admiral Stoker was arrested for attempting to destroy the Vinshari starship while it was defenseless.
    The away team on Pasqual-1 returned to the USS Sekhmet to find it under attack. Conrak and Val rushed to battle stations on the bridge where Admiral Stoker and Captain Vrenta were keeping things under control so far.

    The enemy ship was a strange slender design with masts holding up sails. It fired weapons with physical projectiles with surprising effectiveness. Conrak and Val coordinated to return fire quick, dealing some damage to its shields. The Enemy fired a harpoon, which pierced a hole in the shielding and began draining energy from the Sekhmet's hull. Bov organized an emergy response team to dislodge the harpoon.

    The enemy ship retreated behind the planet and came swinging around the other side, but Conrak and Val were ready for them. Bov diverted Reserve power to the weapons and Conrak disabled their shield. Then Val ordered a torpedo to be fired. Admiral Stoker doubled down on the order and asked for a full salvo of torpedoes, enough to completely obliterate the enemy ship. Conrak did fire a Salvo but intentionally missed with several of the torpedoes.

    Captain Vrenta stood up against the admiral, saying that firing on a defenseless ship was ground to arrest the Admiral, and that's exactly what happened. Further, the bridge crew from the enemy vessel was beamed onto the Sekhmet and arrested for interrogation. They had blood red skin and high collar bones. They called themselves the Vinshari.
  • Sekhmet Log 6 - Life and Soul:
    Stardate 67314, Session: 9
    The crew investigated a strange holodeck program found on Admiral Stoker’s shuttle. On it, they found a 1950’s super spy scenario in which Stoker had hidden detailed logs of her solo adventures in the Shackleton Expanse. They found them disguised as the blueprints for atomic weapons in the private mansion of German weapons dealer.
    As Star Fleet prepared for Admiral Stoker’s trial, Bov and Val were picking apart her shuttle, looking for hidden evidence. They found a holodeck program, created on the shuttle itself during her solo excursion into the Shackleton Expanse.

    The program was a pulpy 1950's themed spy mission, where the party was tasked with recovering atomic bomb blueprints from a German weapons dealer named Mr. Halgrave.

    Halgrave was hosting a birthday party, where multiple other people were trying to acquire the plans. Most of the other guests were invited by Halgrave for his supposed birthday party. But Allied intelligence suspected the party was cover for an auction.

    At the party, Halgrave was no where to be seen. But the crew met with several would-be buyers at the auction. Val spoke with some of the guests along with Bov and Relek Tovan, trying to guess what Stoker's intention was. Meanwhile, Conrak infiltrated the upper floor and found a stockpile of machine guns. Security guards arrived and Conrak took them all out.

    In the ballroom, Val through a explosive pen at Halgrave, injuring him. Conrak was able to finish Hagrave off and took a key off the body. The key opened Halgrave's office, which had the atomic weapons blueprints on them

    But the blueprints were not blueprints at all. Instead, there was a large conspiracy board full of logs, maps, and other data pertaining to Stoker’s solo adventure into the Shackleton Expanse, including a star map of suspected “Facility” locations and first-contact conversations with people calling themselves Akaru and Vinshari. In these conversation, Stoker attempted to threaten these new civilizations with the power of the Federation unless they revealed any ancient alien artifacts or facilities on their planets.
  • Sekhmet Log 7 - Border Dispute:
    Stardate 67333, Session: 10
    The USS Sekhmet was sent to rendezvous with the Nightingale but arrived to find it crippled by a Romulan Warbird inside the Neutral Zone. The Sekhmet launched a surprise attack and disabled the Warbird’s shields. During the battle, a Starfleet shuttle launched from the Nightingale and fled to the Romulans, forcing the Sekhmet to destroy it before it could escape. The pilot was revealed to be T’Lek, a Vulcan officer now presumed to be a traitor.
    The USS Sekhmet was sent to rendevous with the USS Nightingale to investigate what Admiral Stoker had done there before going into the Shackleton expanse.

    The Nightingale was a survey ship that had done a pass into the shackleton expanse along a long patrol. They were currently doing a sweep along the Romulan Neutral Zone.

    Before the Sekhmet could arrive, the Nightingale unexpectedly veered into the neutral zone. They were attacked by a Romulan Warbird, which disabled the ship and killed half their crew, with another 25% severely injured.

    However, the Romulans claimed to have taken a hit to their shields and were performing repairs before they would take the Nightingale and its crew as prisoners of war.

    The Sekhmet arrived and managed to disguise it's arrival in a nebula. From here they observed the scene. The Romulan Warbird was not truly damaged. The Sekhmet's crew assumed the Romulans may be recovering an agent or or asset.

    The Sekhmet launched a surprise attack against the Warbird, and managed to truly disable it's shields with a torpedo salvo and then move into a position to protect the Nightingale. The Warbird attempted to defend itself and repair it's shields, but it was too late.

    In the middle of the fight, a shuttle departed from the Nightingale careening towards the Warbird. It moved fast enough that the Sekhmet was unable to capture it in a tractor beam, and retreated into the wings of the Warbird. With no other way to stop the shuttle and fears that critical information could get into Romulan hands, the Sekhmet fired upon the shuttle itself destroying it and killing it's only crew member.

    The Romulan Warbird hailed the Sekhmet and said its goodbyes. It was none other than Sub-Commander Thal'vaek.

    With the Romulans taken care of, the Sekhmet performed basic repairs and relief for the survivors on the Nightingale. Their computers had been disabled by their captain using the Romulan Protocal, and a supposed Vulcan Starfleet Lieutenant named T'Lek had been working to restore them to assist with repairs. However, soon after computers were back online, during the fight, the T'Lek took the shuttle to the Romulans. It's currently unknown what intel T'Lek intended to give to Thal'vaek.
